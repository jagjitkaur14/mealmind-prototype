📋 Assignment Documentation

Part 1: Target User & Problem Definition

Primary Persona — "The Overwhelmed Professional"

Meet Priya, 29 — a working professional in Hyderabad who cooks for 2. She gets home by 7pm, doesn't want to spend more than 30 minutes cooking, has dietary preferences (vegetarian), and hates wasting groceries she buys but forgets to use.

Age 25–40Working ProfessionalTime-poorHealth-consciousBudget-aware

Core Problems Identified:

  • Decision fatigue: "What should I cook?" happens daily and wastes mental energy
  • Pantry waste: 30% of groceries expire unused due to poor planning
  • Diet drift: Without a plan, users default to unhealthy, quick options
  • Shopping inefficiency: Buying items they already have; forgetting what's needed
  • One-size-fits-all: Existing tools don't respect time constraints or pantry stock

Problem Statement: Busy individuals need a smart meal planning tool that adapts to their available time, dietary needs, and pantry contents — so that they can eat well without spending hours planning or wasting food.

Key Decision Points:

  • If pantry >70% match → prioritise those recipes to reduce shopping
  • If dietary restriction conflicts with recipe → auto-exclude and flag

Part 3: Prototype — What Was Built

This prototype replicates the Lovable-style interactive prototype across 6 screens:

  • Dashboard: 7-day calendar grid with Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner slots, stats bar, quick actions
  • Generate Plan: Step-by-step plan builder with pantry + preferences context
  • Pantry Manager: Add/remove pantry items by category; stock level visualization
  • Shopping List: Auto-generated checklist by category; checkoff, export, share
  • Preferences: Dietary toggles, time slider, cuisine selector, allergen manager
  • Meal Cards: Click any meal to open detailed modal with ingredients, time, swap
HTML/CSS/JS No dependencies Fully Interactive Dark UI Theme Mobile Responsive

Part 4: Test & Refine — Feedback Framework

Usability Test Plan:

  • Task 1: "Set your dietary preference to vegetarian and max 30 min cook time"
  • Task 2: "Add 5 pantry items you have at home right now"
  • Task 3: "Generate a weekly meal plan and swap Tuesday dinner"
  • Task 4: "Export your shopping list"

Metrics to track:

  • Task completion rate (target: >85%)
  • Time-on-task for plan generation (<2 minutes)
  • NPS/CSAT after first week of use
  • Pantry match % week over week

Iteration priorities (post-testing):

  • V1 → V2: Add meal rating; use ratings to improve future suggestions
  • V2 → V3: Calorie/nutrition tracking per day
  • V3 → V4: AI recipe suggestions from photo of pantry
  • Future: Social sharing, family multi-user, integration with grocery delivery apps
